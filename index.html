How it works

Short interview, close first draft.

Most of the work is mine, not yours. I only need your engineer for one conversation.

  1. Brief

    You point me at the project or product and the engineer who did the work. I read the background — datasheet, standard, competitor spec — before we speak.

  2. Interview

    30–45 minutes with your engineer. I get the numbers, the constraints and the decisions that didn’t make it into the report.

  3. Draft

    A first draft written to survive technical review, so your engineering lead isn’t rewriting it. You get the file within 10 working days.

  4. Revisions

    Two rounds of revisions included, then final files in the format you need. Copyright transfers to you on payment.

About

An engineer who writes, not a writer who Googles.

I’m Dylan Stewart. I have a background in Aerospace Engineering, and I spend my time doing the kind of work your customers do — reading specifications, chasing root causes, and writing things that have to be exactly right.

That’s the useful part. Most copywriters selling to technical companies have to be taught the difference between a requirement and a specification, or why qualification testing matters more than a marketing claim.
